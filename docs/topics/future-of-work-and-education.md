# Future of Work & Education

## Description
Work and learning are two of the most consequential domains AI is reshaping. Entire job categories are shifting, the skills that earn a living are changing faster than educational systems can adapt, and the question of what humans are for in an economy of increasingly capable machines is becoming urgent rather than hypothetical. Salons here explore not just what's changing, but how individuals and institutions can navigate it — and what we want to preserve.

**Conversation Topics**
- Which jobs and skills are most exposed to AI automation — and what "exposed" actually means for the people in them
- The difference between AI augmenting workers and AI replacing them, and who captures the gains in each case
- How education systems need to change when AI can write, code, analyze, and tutor — and what schools are for when information is free
- Whether a universal basic income or other social policies are prerequisites for an AI-abundant future
- What "having a career" means for someone entering the workforce today, and how to build durable skills

**Evocative Questions**
- If AI can do your job as well as you within five years, what would you want to do instead?
- What's the difference between a job being automated and a person being made obsolete?
- Should we be trying to prepare people for the AI economy, or trying to shape the AI economy around people?
- What would you teach a child today to prepare them for a world you can't predict?
- If work is no longer necessary for survival, what gives a life structure and meaning?
