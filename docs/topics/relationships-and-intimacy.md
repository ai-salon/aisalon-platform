# Relationships & Intimacy

## Description
Human connection — love, friendship, loneliness, belonging — is being reshaped by AI in ways both subtle and profound. Companion apps fill solitude, dating platforms optimize attraction, and AI therapists offer availability that human ones cannot. Salons in this topic sit with the tension between convenience and depth: what we actually need from other people, and whether AI can ever provide it.

**Conversation Topics**
- How dating apps and AI matching change the experience of attraction, courtship, and commitment
- AI companions and their role in addressing loneliness — genuine support or a substitute that reduces motivation for real connection
- The effect of social media and algorithmic feeds on how we perceive relationships and compare ourselves to others
- What intimacy requires: vulnerability, risk, presence, imperfection — and which of those AI can simulate
- How relationships change when one partner uses AI for advice, companionship, or emotional processing

**Evocative Questions**
- Could a relationship with an AI that knows you deeply be more intimate than one with a person who doesn't?
- What do we owe each other in relationships that we can never owe a machine?
- Is loneliness a signal that needs to be addressed, or a condition we've learned to pathologize?
- If AI could perfectly simulate the feeling of being loved, would that be enough?
- How do you stay genuinely curious about a person in a world where you can look up everything about them?
