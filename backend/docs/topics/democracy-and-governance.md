# Democracy & Governance

## Description
AI is arriving into political systems that were not designed for it — slow-moving institutions confronting fast-moving technology, democratic processes built for deliberation facing tools that move at machine speed. Salons in this topic explore how societies govern AI, how AI changes governance, and what it means for democratic legitimacy, national power, and global equity when the most consequential technologies are controlled by a handful of companies and countries.

**Conversation Topics**
- The mismatch between AI's pace of development and democracy's deliberative speed
- Who gets to set the rules — companies, governments, international bodies, or citizens — and what accountability looks like
- AI as an instrument of national power: the US-China competition, export controls, and the risk of racing toward unsafe systems
- How surveillance, disinformation, and algorithmic manipulation threaten electoral integrity
- The governance gap for the rest of the world: countries that didn't build the technology but live under its effects

**Evocative Questions**
- Can democratic institutions govern something they struggle to understand?
- When AI optimizes for efficiency, what democratic values does it quietly discard?
- Is there a version of the US-China AI race that doesn't end badly for someone?
- Who should decide what values are encoded into the systems that govern our information?
- What would it look like for a country to develop AI in a way that prioritizes its own citizens over competitive advantage?
