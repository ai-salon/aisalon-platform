# Science Fiction & Long-Term Futures

## Description
What kind of future is actually possible — and what kind do we want? Science fiction has long been the laboratory for exploring AI futures, from utopian post-scarcity to dystopian control. Salons here take the long view: not just what AI will do in five years, but what human civilization might look like in fifty. These conversations often start with a story and end with a question about values — what we want to preserve, and what we're willing to let go.

**Conversation Topics**
- Classic and contemporary AI narratives in science fiction — what they got right, what they missed, and what they reveal about our fears
- Longevity and life extension: what changes about how we live when death is optional or postponed indefinitely
- Digital immortality: preserving consciousness, AI "copies" of the dead, and what we owe to future simulations of ourselves
- Autonomous scientific discovery: AI that generates hypotheses, runs experiments, and advances knowledge faster than humans can follow
- The post-scarcity question: if AI produces material abundance, what happens to inequality, status, and purpose?

**Evocative Questions**
- Which sci-fi AI future feels most plausible to you right now — and which feels most desirable?
- If you could upload your consciousness and live indefinitely as software, would you? What would you lose?
- Should we build AI that can do science autonomously, or does the process of discovery matter as much as the result?
- What would you want the world to look like in 100 years — and what role does AI play in getting there?
- Are we better at imagining AI dystopias than AI utopias? Why?
