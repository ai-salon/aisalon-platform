# AI in Society & Global Context

## Description
AI is not experienced uniformly. Its benefits and harms land differently depending on geography, economic position, and which communities built the systems in the first place. Salons in this topic examine AI as a social force: how it interacts with children and development, how it plays out in regions outside Silicon Valley, and how we build systems that serve humanity broadly rather than concentrating advantage among those who already have it.

**Conversation Topics**
- How children are growing up alongside AI — cognitive development, education, social skills, and the ethics of exposing young people to AI companions
- Africa and the Global South as AI markets, producers, and subjects — not just recipients of technology built elsewhere
- The concentration of AI capability in a handful of companies and countries, and what it means for everyone else
- Whether AI can help "leapfrog" development (as mobile did for banking in Africa) or tends to entrench existing inequalities
- How cultural context shapes what "beneficial AI" means — values that seem universal often aren't

**Evocative Questions**
- Who gets to decide what values are built into AI systems that affect billions of people who had no voice in designing them?
- Could AI development actually be led from Lagos, Bangalore, or Nairobi — and what would that change?
- What's the responsibility of someone who builds AI toward the communities most affected by it?
- Is "leapfrogging" with AI realistic, or does it tend to import the biases and power structures of whoever built the system?
- What do children need from adults — and from technology — that no AI system can provide?
