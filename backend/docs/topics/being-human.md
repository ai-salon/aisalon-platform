# Being Human

## Description
What does it mean to be human when machines can think, create, and converse? This topic explores the interior territory: consciousness, identity, selfhood, and the qualities we consider distinctly ours. As AI blurs the lines around cognition, creativity, and even personhood, salons in this space ask participants to examine their own nature — not just what AI can do, but what it reveals about us.

**Conversation Topics**
- The relationship between language, thought, and identity — and what it means that AI is fluent in one without the others
- How embodied experience (the body, emotion, sensation) shapes human understanding in ways AI lacks
- Whether there is a coherent "self" that persists through time, and how AI companions or digital twins challenge that sense
- The difference between simulating understanding and actually having it — and whether the distinction matters
- What cognitive capacities we risk losing as AI handles more of our thinking

**Evocative Questions**
- If an AI can pass every behavioral test for consciousness, does it matter whether it's "really" conscious?
- What would you lose if AI could do everything you do — but better?
- Are the things that make you uniquely you learnable, or are they irreducibly yours?
- Does using AI to think make you more yourself, or less?
- What does it mean to "understand" something, and can a system without experience ever truly understand?
