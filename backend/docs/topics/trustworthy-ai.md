# Trustworthy AI

## Description
As AI systems become more capable and more consequential, questions of trust, safety, and alignment move from philosophy to engineering to policy. What does it take to build AI we can actually rely on? Salons in this topic examine the mechanisms — technical, social, and institutional — that make AI beneficial rather than harmful, and the tensions between moving fast and getting it right.

**Conversation Topics**
- What "alignment" means in practice: aligning AI with whose values, determined how, and enforced by whom
- The spectrum from near-term harms (bias, misinformation, surveillance) to longer-term risks (autonomous agents, power concentration, misalignment at scale)
- How companies, governments, and civil society each play a role — and where each falls short
- The tension between safety and competitiveness: does slowing down for safety cede ground to less careful actors?
- Interpretability, auditing, and red-teaming: what tools exist to understand what AI is actually doing

**Evocative Questions**
- What would it take for you to genuinely trust an AI system with an important decision in your life?
- Is the "race to the top" or the "race to the bottom" more likely in AI development, and why?
- Who is responsible when an AI system causes harm — the developer, the deployer, the user?
- Can you build safe AI without understanding how it works internally?
- What would a trustworthy AI future actually look like in practice, not just in principle?
